Dimebag Darrell

With electrifying riffs and screaming solos, Dimebag Darrell reshaped heavy metal. As Pantera's Lead guitarist, he was able to create riffs that were heavy and melodic at the same time. Techniques such as screeching pinch harmonics, dive bombs, and accurate rapid picking became his bread n' butter. Hits such as Cowboys from H**l, Floods, and Walk each became one of metal's most iconic songs.

Jimi Hendrix

With heartfelt solos and revolutionary sound, Jimi Hendrix reinvented rock. Rising from Seattle, he combined blues, rock, and psychedelia to form his own unique sound that redefined guitar itself by popularizing the use of effects such as Wah, Fuzz, and Distortion. His undeniable charisma and skill made him one of music's most iconic figures, and arguably the greatest guitarist of all time.

Eddie Van Halen

One of guitar's most innovative figures. Eddie Van Halen revolutionized rock with his rapid-tapping, soaring harmonics, lightning-fast picking, and fearless experimentation. Blending technique with style and infectious energy, he reshaped the sound of the modern guitar and popularized shredding.
