Step 1: Learn the Parts

  • Identify the guitar body, neck, and headstock
  • Locate the tuning pegs, frets, and bridge (refer to the image)
  • Learn the names of each string, from top to bottom: (E, A, D, G, B, e) ('Eddie Ate Dynamite Good Bye eddie')

Step 2: Learn the Stance

  • Sit on a stable chair with no armrests
  • Place the guitar on your dominant leg
  • Hold the neck using your non-dominant hand
  • Keep your back straight and shoulders relaxed

Step 3: Tune It

  • Use a digital tuner or tuning app, I recommend the GuitarTuna app
  • Start from the lowest (E) string
  • Turn the tuning pegs slowly until each string matches the correct pitch

Step 4: Learn the Pick

  • Hold the pick between your thumb and index finger using your dominant hand
  • Keep a relaxed grip, don't hold the pick too tightly
  • Practice picking down on each string slowly

Step 5: Practice Fretting

  • Press strings just behind the frets, not on top, not way behind
  • Use your fingertips and keep your thumb behind the neck
  • Practice pressing and playing one note at a time, make sure the sound is clean and full

Step 6: Play Your First Chord

  • Try a super easy chord like E minor
  • Place your fingers on the second fret of the A and D strings
  • Strum all the strings gently from top to bottom, make sure the sound is clean and full
  • After completing this, you may practice more chords and basic strumming patterns. Stay consistent, you can do it!
